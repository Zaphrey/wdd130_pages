Bringing the right hiking supplies is very important when going on a hiking trip. Make sure you plan out your trip to the smallest detail and have the proper supplies for the trail you’ve chosen. For example, if it’s a sunny trail you may want to bring sunscreen or a sun hat. If the area is more wooded, bring some bug spray. Always be prepared and be aware of where you are going.

First Aid Kit

Bringing a first aid kit is one of the most essential items you can bring on a hike. It's important to treat any wounds that you may have gotten on a hiking trip. Your first aid kit should contain items to treat blisters, clean up any wounds or cuts, and a tool to remove ticks.

Hiking Footwear

Bringing the proper footwear is important if you want keep traction and avoid getting blisters while hiking. Hiking boots or any outdoor oriented shoes are recommended. If you don't have any of those you can wear running shoes or sneakers, although they are not recommended.

Knife or Multitool

Bringing a knife or multitool is also a very essential item to bring while going outdoors. It can be used to prepare food, cut cord, and can even save you in a life threatening situation.

Plenty of Food and Water

Bringing plenty of food and water is important for hikes. Bringing food on the way can help you replenish some energy, and water helps keep you from being dehydrated.

Bug Repellant

Bringing bug repellant is important when hiking in Georgia. Bug repellant can repel ticks and mosquitos which may carry harmful diseases on them.

Trekking Poles

Trekking poles aren't essential, but are useful to bring on more strenuous hikes. They are useful for stability when crossing streams or rough terrain. Trekking poles can also help with shifting some of your body weight onto the poles, minimizing the pain you may get from walking up or down steep slopes.
